To begin, thread your needle with the desired length of thread. Make sure to leave a tail of at least two inches at the end of the thread. To start your stitch, bring the needle up from the back of the fabric and pull the thread through. Then, insert the needle back into the fabric a short distance away from the starting point. Pull the thread through and repeat this process until you reach the end of the line. To finish the stitch, bring the needle up from the back of the fabric and pull the thread through. Then, insert the needle back into the fabric a short distance away from the end of the line and pull the thread through. Finally, tie a knot at the end of the thread to secure the stitch.

To end your embroidery stitches, bring the needle up from the back of the fabric and pull the thread through. Then, insert the needle back into the fabric a short distance away from the end of the line and pull the thread through. Finally, make a loop with the thread and pass the needle through the loop. Pull the thread tight to secure the stitch. To finish, snip off the excess thread.

With these simple steps, you can easily learn how to start and end embroidery stitches. With practice and patience, you can create beautiful and intricate designs that will last for years to come.
