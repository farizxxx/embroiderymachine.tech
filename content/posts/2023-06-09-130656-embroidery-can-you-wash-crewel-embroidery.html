Can You Wash Crewel Embroidery? Crewel embroidery is a type of decorative needlework that uses wool thread to create intricate designs. It is a popular craft that has been around for centuries, and it is often used to create beautiful wall hangings, pillows, and other home decor items. But when it comes to cleaning and caring for crewel embroidery, there are a few things to keep in mind.

Crewel embroidery should never be washed in a washing machine. The wool thread used in crewel embroidery is delicate and can easily become damaged in a washing machine. Instead, it should be hand washed in cold water with a mild detergent. It is also important to avoid using any bleach or fabric softener, as these can damage the wool thread. After washing, the embroidery should be laid flat to dry.

It is also important to avoid ironing crewel embroidery. The heat from an iron can cause the wool thread to shrink and distort the design. If the embroidery needs to be pressed, it should be done on the wrong side with a damp cloth. This will help to keep the design intact and looking its best.
