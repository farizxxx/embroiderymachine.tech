Embroidery is an art form that has been around for centuries. It is a craft that involves stitching fabric with thread to create beautiful designs. Embroidery stitches are the foundation of this craft, and they come in a variety of shapes and sizes. Each stitch has its own unique purpose and can be used to create different effects. From simple running stitches to complex chain stitches, there is a stitch for every project.

Running Stitch - This is the most basic of all embroidery stitches and is used to outline shapes or to create a border. It is a simple stitch that is made up of small, even stitches that are worked in a straight line. It is often used to outline shapes or to create a border.

Back Stitch - This stitch is used to create outlines and to join two pieces of fabric together. It is made up of small, even stitches that are worked in a backward motion. It is a strong stitch that is often used to create outlines or to join two pieces of fabric together.
