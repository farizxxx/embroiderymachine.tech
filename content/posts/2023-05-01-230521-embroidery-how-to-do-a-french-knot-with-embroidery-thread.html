How to do a French Knot with Embroidery Thread

A French knot is a type of embroidery stitch used to create a small, raised dot. It is a versatile stitch that can be used to create a variety of patterns and designs. To create a French knot with embroidery thread, you will need a needle, embroidery thread, and a piece of fabric.

Begin by threading the needle with the embroidery thread. Make sure to leave a tail of thread at the end of the needle. Next, bring the needle up through the fabric from the back side. Wrap the thread around the needle two or three times, depending on the size of the knot you want to create. Then, insert the needle back into the fabric, close to where it came up. Pull the thread tight to secure the knot.

Continue to create French knots in the desired pattern. To finish, tie off the thread on the back side of the fabric. Trim any excess thread. With practice, you will be able to create beautiful French knot designs with embroidery thread.
