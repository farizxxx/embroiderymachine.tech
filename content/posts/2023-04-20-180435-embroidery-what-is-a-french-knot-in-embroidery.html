What is a French Knot in Embroidery? A French knot is a type of embroidery stitch used to create a small, raised dot. It is a versatile stitch that can be used to create a variety of textures and patterns. The French knot is a popular choice for adding texture and dimension to embroidery projects.

To create a French knot, the thread is wrapped around the needle two or three times before being pulled through the fabric. The number of times the thread is wrapped around the needle will determine the size of the knot. The more times the thread is wrapped, the larger the knot will be. The French knot can be used to create a variety of patterns, from small dots to larger, more intricate designs.
