Embroidery is a great way to add a unique touch to any project. Whether you’re making a quilt, a pillow, or a wall hanging, adding a few simple embroidery flowers can really make your project stand out. In this blog post, we’ll show you how to make a simple embroidery flower that you can use to embellish any project.

How to Make a Simple Embroidery Flower

To make a simple embroidery flower, you’ll need a few basic supplies: embroidery floss, a needle, and a piece of fabric. Start by cutting a length of embroidery floss that’s about 18 inches long. Thread the needle with the floss and tie a knot at the end. Then, make a small loop in the fabric and insert the needle through the loop. Pull the needle and floss through the loop and then make another loop. Continue making loops until you have a flower shape. When you’re finished, tie a knot at the end of the floss and cut off the excess. Your simple embroidery flower is now complete!

Embroidery flowers are a great way to add a unique touch to any project. With just a few simple supplies and a bit of time, you can create a beautiful flower that will add a special touch to any project. So grab your supplies and get stitching!
