Embroidery and crewel are two distinct forms of needlework that have been used for centuries to create beautiful and intricate designs. Embroidery is a form of decorative needlework that uses a needle and thread to create a design on fabric. Crewel is a type of embroidery that uses wool yarn to create a design on fabric. Both embroidery and crewel are used to create beautiful and intricate designs, but there are some key differences between the two.

The main difference between embroidery and crewel is the type of thread used. Embroidery uses a variety of threads, including cotton, silk, and rayon, while crewel uses only wool yarn. Embroidery is also typically done on a much finer scale than crewel, with smaller stitches and more intricate designs. Crewel, on the other hand, is usually done on a larger scale, with larger stitches and bolder designs. Embroidery is also usually done on a much lighter fabric than crewel, such as linen or muslin, while crewel is usually done on a heavier fabric such as velvet or tweed. Additionally, embroidery is usually done with a single color of thread, while crewel is usually done with multiple colors of wool yarn.
