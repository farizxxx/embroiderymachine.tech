How is Sashiko Embroidery Done?

Sashiko embroidery is a traditional Japanese hand-stitching technique that has been used for centuries to create beautiful and intricate designs. It is a form of decorative stitching that uses a running stitch to create patterns and designs on fabric. The stitches are usually done in white thread on a dark fabric, but can also be done in other colors. The stitches are usually done in a geometric pattern, but can also be done in a more free-form style.

To do sashiko embroidery, you will need a needle, thread, and fabric. You will also need a thimble to protect your finger from the needle. Begin by threading the needle with the thread and knotting the end. Then, start stitching the pattern on the fabric. The stitches should be small and even, and should be done in a single line. You can use a ruler or template to help keep the stitches even. Once the pattern is complete, knot the thread and cut off the excess.
